About Naismith's Rule

Devised by Scottish mountaineer William W. Naismith in 1892, this rule of thumb has guided British hillwalkers for over 130 years. It's the standard starting point for trip planning, used by mountain rescue teams, the Mountain Leader syllabus, and countless walking guides.

1 hour per 5 km + 1 hour per 600 m of ascent

Or in imperial: 1 hour per 3 miles + 1 hour per 2,000 ft of ascent.

A sensible caveat

Naismith assumes a reasonably fit walker on good ground in fair weather. The corrections on this page account for fitness, group dynamics, and stops, but they can't account for bogs, scree, winter conditions, heavy packs, or the pint at the finish that slows down the last mile.

Always add a buffer for safety, especially in the hills. Tell someone your route and expected return time. Take a map and compass even if you're using a GPS.
